# MailChimp Content Style Guide — System Index

## Overview

MailChimp's Content Style Guide is a publicly documented writing system used by MailChimp's content, product, and marketing teams. It is one of the most widely referenced content design style guides in the industry — widely cited because it applies cross-system writing principles to product copy, marketing, and UX writing at scale.

Unlike most design systems in this KB, MailChimp's guide focuses exclusively on content: voice, tone, grammar, mechanics, and accessible writing. It does not cover visual design, color, or component patterns. That makes it a primary reference for content design decisions rather than visual design system decisions.

When to reference this system: when establishing a voice and tone guide for a new product, auditing UI copy for consistency, or defining writing mechanics rules.

---

## What's in this section

- **voice-and-tone** — MailChimp's four voice characteristics (plainspoken, genuine, translators, dry humor), the tone framework, and quality standards for content.
- **grammar-and-mechanics** — Capitalization, punctuation, numbers, pronouns, and formatting rules.
- **writing-for-accessibility** — Accessible writing patterns: alt text, header structure, link text, form labels, and plain language guidelines.
